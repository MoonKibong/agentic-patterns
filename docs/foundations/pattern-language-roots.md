# Pattern Language Roots

Agentic Patterns is not meant to be a prompt trick collection. Its deeper model comes from the
pattern-language tradition: name recurring problems, describe the forces that make them difficult,
record a reusable core solution, and make that solution communicable across people and tools.

## Christopher Alexander

Christopher Alexander and collaborators developed the architectural pattern-language tradition in
works such as *A Pattern Language: Towns, Buildings, Construction* and *The Timeless Way of
Building*. Their patterns described recurring problems in towns, buildings, and rooms, together with
solutions that could be reused without being copied mechanically.

The important idea for this project is not the building domain itself. It is the social and cognitive
function of a pattern language:

- A pattern gives a name to a recurring design situation.
- It records the forces and tradeoffs that make the situation hard.
- It describes a solution shape, not a rigid script.
- It lets non-experts and experts communicate about design decisions.
- Patterns compose into a language, so larger designs can be built from smaller named moves.

That last point matters. A catalog of isolated tips is not yet a language. A pattern language becomes
useful when patterns call upon each other, constrain each other, and give a community shared words
for repeated work.

## GoF Design Patterns

The software design-pattern movement adapted this idea to object-oriented software. The Gang of Four
book, *Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software*, made the format widely known
to software engineers by cataloging named recurring solutions such as Factory Method, Adapter,
Composite, Decorator, Observer, and Strategy.

The GoF catalog made several practical contributions that matter here:

- Patterns became a communication tool for design review.
- Pattern names compressed complex design discussions.
- Reusable structure was separated from one-off implementation details.
- Tradeoffs and applicability became part of engineering judgment.
- Shared vocabulary improved collaboration across teams.

The lesson is not that agentic workflows should copy GoF categories. The lesson is that repeated
practice becomes more powerful when it is named, structured, criticized, and taught.

## Why Agentic Work Needs Pattern Language

Agentic coding has made the need visible first. Developers repeatedly rediscover the same workflow
questions:

- What context should the agent receive?
- When should the agent plan before acting?
- Which tools may it use, and with what approval boundaries?
- When does a reviewer or critique loop pay for itself?
- What state must survive chat compaction, handoff, or restart?

The same questions appear outside coding:

- Image generation needs briefs, references, variation control, critique, and selection.
- Writing needs voice, outline, continuity, critique, and revision.
- Design generation needs constraints, feasibility review, artifact handoff, and human approval.
- Video and media work needs storyboards, continuity ledgers, render gates, and provenance.
- Research needs source ledgers, claim checks, citation anchors, and review loops.

This is why the project uses "human-guided AI work" rather than only "agentic coding." Coding is the
first mature domain, but the underlying pattern language is broader.

## Patterns As Memetic Operational Knowledge

Patterns are memetic in the practical sense: they are compact cultural units that can be transmitted,
modified, and recombined. A good pattern helps a community remember a useful solution without
requiring every person to repeat the original discovery.

Agentic patterns are especially memetic because they mediate between humans and AI systems. They do
not merely tell humans what to do. They also shape what an AI agent can recognize, choose, and
execute.

In this project, a pattern should help answer:

- What is the recurring situation?
- What forces make it hard?
- What reusable move helps?
- What evidence or examples support it?
- When should it not be used?
- How can an agent recognize and apply it?

## Human Judgment Remains Central

AI systems are powerful pattern matchers, but they do not own the goal. Humans provide purpose,
taste, memory, social context, ethical judgment, and the ability to decide when a pattern should be
broken.

The aim of Agentic Patterns is therefore not to automate human judgment away. It is to make the
reusable parts of human judgment legible enough that people and agents can collaborate better.
